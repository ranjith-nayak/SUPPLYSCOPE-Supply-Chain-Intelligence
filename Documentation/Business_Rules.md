# Business Rules - SUPPLYSCOPE

## 1. Company Overview

### Company Name

Redwood Logistics

### Industry

Supply Chain & Logistics Operations

### Business Description

Redwood Logistics is a nationwide logistics and distribution company responsible for managing supplier relationships, inventory operations, warehouse management, transportation networks, and customer order fulfillment across multiple regions.

The company aims to improve operational efficiency through data-driven decision-making, inventory optimization, supplier performance management, logistics monitoring, and demand forecasting.

---

## 2. Project Overview

### Project Name

SUPPLYSCOPE

### Project Description

SUPPLYSCOPE is a Supply Chain Intelligence & Predictive Operations Platform designed to analyze and optimize the end-to-end supply chain operations of Redwood Logistics.

The platform leverages SQL, Python, and Tableau to provide actionable insights across inventory management, logistics operations, supplier performance, warehouse efficiency, and demand forecasting.

---

## 3. Supply Chain Flow

The supply chain process follows the structure below:

Supplier → Warehouse → Order Fulfillment → Shipment → Customer

Each stage generates operational data that can be analyzed to improve efficiency, reduce costs, and enhance customer satisfaction.

---

## 4. Business Objectives

The primary objectives of Redwood Logistics are:

* Improve on-time delivery performance
* Reduce inventory shortages
* Optimize warehouse utilization
* Improve supplier reliability
* Minimize logistics costs
* Forecast future demand accurately
* Support data-driven operational decision-making

## 6. Supplier Network

Suppliers provide products to Redwood Logistics warehouses.

### Business Rules

* A supplier may supply multiple products.
* Multiple suppliers may supply products across different categories.
* Suppliers have varying lead times.
* Suppliers have varying reliability scores.
* Supplier delays can impact inventory availability and order fulfillment.

### Key Metrics

* Supplier Reliability (%)
* Average Lead Time
* Defect Rate
* Supplier Performance Score

Warehouses are responsible for storing inventory and fulfilling customer orders.

### Regions

* North
* South

### Business Rules

* Products are stored across multiple warehouses.
* Warehouses have defined storage capacities.

## 8. Inventory Management

Inventory is monitored to maintain product availability while minimizing excess stock.

### Business Rules

* Every product has a stock level.
* Every product has a reorder point.
* Inventory below the reorder point indicates stock risk.
* Excess inventory increases holding costs.
* Inventory shortages may impact customer fulfillment.

### Key Metrics

* Inventory Turnover
* Stock Availability (%)
* Dead Stock Value
* Stockout Risk
* Reorder Quantity

---

## 9. Logistics Operations

Logistics operations manage the transportation of products from warehouses to customers.

### Transportation Modes

* Air
* Road
* Rail
* Express Courier
* Standard Shipping

### Business Rules

* Transportation costs vary by shipping mode.
* Delivery time varies by shipping mode.
* Delayed shipments negatively impact customer satisfaction.
* Regional performance may differ based on logistics conditions.

### Key Metrics

* Average Delivery Time
* Transportation Cost

## 10. Customer Fulfillment

Customer orders represent the final stage of the supply chain.

### Business Rules

* Orders must be fulfilled using available inventory.
* Inventory shortages may delay order fulfillment.
* Delayed fulfillment may impact delivery performance.
* Fulfillment efficiency affects customer experience.

### Key Metrics

* Total Orders
* Fulfillment Rate (%)
* Average Order Processing Time
* Revenue

## 11. Demand Forecasting

Historical sales and demand data are used to forecast future inventory requirements.

### Business Rules

* Product demand varies over time.
* Seasonal trends may influence demand.
* Forecasting helps prevent stockouts and overstocking.
* Inventory planning should be aligned with forecasted demand.

### Key Metrics

* Forecasted Demand
* Predicted Inventory Requirement
* Future Stockout Risk
* Restocking Recommendation

## 12. Core Business Relationships

The operational performance of Redwood Logistics is influenced by the following chain of events:

Supplier Delay
→ Inventory Shortage
→ Order Fulfillment Delay
→ Shipment Delay
→ Customer Impact

Understanding these relationships is critical for identifying operational bottlenecks and improving overall supply chain efficiency.

## 13. Key Business Questions

SUPPLYSCOPE is designed to answer the following questions:

### Executive Control Tower

* Is the supply chain operating efficiently?
* Are delivery targets being achieved?
* Which regions are underperforming?

### Inventory Intelligence

* Which products are overstocked?
* Which products are at risk of stockout?
* Which products move fastest?

### Logistics Intelligence

* Which routes experience the highest delays?
* Which transportation modes are most efficient?
* Where are logistics costs increasing?

### Supplier Intelligence

* Which suppliers are most reliable?
* Which suppliers contribute to operational delays?
* Which suppliers have quality issues?

### Predictive Intelligence

* What demand is expected next month?
* Which products require replenishment?
* Which inventory risks should management prepare for?
